# Sodium Hypochlorite (NaClO)

Sodium Hypochlorite is the primary disinfectant used at the Albany Water Department. It is a clear, light yellow-green liquid with a strong chlorine odor.

## Chemical Properties
* **Trade Name:** Hypo, Bleach
* **Strength:** Delivered at **15%** available chlorine.
* **Specific Gravity:** ~1.21 (Heavier than water).
* **pH:** > 12 (Highly Alkaline/Caustic).

## Application Points
| Location | Purpose | Dosage Range |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Raw Water** | Pre-oxidation & Algae Control | 0.5 - 1.0 mg/L |
| **Filtered Water** | Primary Disinfection | 1.5 - 2.5 mg/L |
| **Reservoirs** | Residual Maintenance | As Needed |

!!! danger "Safety Warning"
    **NEVER** mix Sodium Hypochlorite with acids (like fluoride or PAC). This reaction releases **Chlorine Gas**, which can be fatal.
